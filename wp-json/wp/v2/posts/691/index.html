We’re winding down our first winter as fulltimers and looking forward to several months of travel; exploring new places.\u00a0 We divided our winter between Dickinson and Rockport, Texas.\u00a0 Dickinson is close to family and friends (and doctors) and Rockport is a nice Winter Texan destination.\u00a0 Obviously, there are advantages to being in both places.<\/p>\n

During these months we’ve taken on several projects: including the maintenance, repair, and upgrade varieties.\u00a0 Here’s a wrap up of those projects.<\/p>\n

Maintenance:<\/strong><\/p>\n

    \n
  • Washed camper, waxed front cap<\/li>\n
  • Flushed the water heater<\/li>\n
  • Sanitized the fresh water tank<\/li>\n
  • Replaced the reverse osmosis water filters<\/li>\n
  • Had the pickup transmission and rear end serviced<\/li>\n
  • Had the pickup tires rotated<\/li>\n
  • Refreshed and repaired some of the camper caulking<\/li>\n
  • Checked roof and applied fresh Dicor caulking where needed<\/li>\n
  • Repacked the wheel bearings<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    Repairs:<\/strong><\/p>\n

      \n
    • A biggie: had to replace a bent axle\u00a0(I know exactly where the damage was done)<\/li>\n
    • A few light switches that were getting quite stiff to operate were replaced<\/li>\n
    • Camper floor reinforced where factory had under-engineered it<\/li>\n
    • Recaulked the floor around shower stall where there were some leaks<\/li>\n
    • Pulled the toilet to replace a leaky valve<\/li>\n
    • Changed pin height on camper (needed a bit more space between camper and PU rails)<\/li>\n
    • Fantastic Vent repaired (now opens and closes with thermostat – and rain sensor works)<\/li>\n
    • The two camper 12V batteries replaced<\/li>\n
    • Carbon Monoxide and Smoke Detectors replaced<\/a><\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

      Upgrades:<\/strong><\/p>\n

        \n
      • New water faucets installed<\/a><\/li>\n
      • A new, modern charger\/converter installed<\/a><\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

        Yet to come: the F350 goes in the shop to find a small coolant leak<\/p>\n

        As you can see, there’s been a lot to do! Special thanks to my friend Ron who is the brains behind many of these operations – I couldn’t do the mechanical stuff without him.<\/p>\n

        We love being touring fulltimers, but we’re learning that these rigs need more upkeep than a “sticks and bricks” house does. As you can see, most of the time and effort went into routine maintenance that needs to be done on a regular basis.<\/p>\n

        Now, with Spring upon us, we’re getting excited about our 2014 Adventure!\u00a0 Stay tuned, good times are ahead!<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
