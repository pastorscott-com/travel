<\/p>\n

Our adventures in shopping continue.\u00a0 In traveling to the UK we could bring with us only what could be checked on the plane.\u00a0 Of course, we focused on clothing, personal items, and electronics.\u00a0 That left us needing to purchase many kitchen items (although our new kitchen has all the basics provided).\u00a0 Also, all the minor common items are needed.\u00a0 For instance, I needed a tad of black tape to cover a too-bright LED light on one of our electrical plug adaptors.\u00a0 That required a stop at a small hardware store and paying what to me seemed a high price for a small roll of electrical tape.\u00a0 As we kept adding to our list of “stuff” I told Jackie that what we really needed was a car and a trip to Walmart.\u00a0 However, we have no car and the nearest Walmart is over 3000 miles away!\u00a0 We haven’t found a good substitute for Walmart, but if we did we would have to get there by public transportation and then carry everything home on the bus.<\/p>\n

So\u2026Amazon!\u00a0 It’s just a click away, the prices are good, and they will deliver in a few days.\u00a0 I changed my Amazon address to their UK version and I’m good to go.\u00a0 We’re ordering stuff we’d never consider ordering online before!\u00a0 From what I can see here on campus we have lots of company.\u00a0 The Amazon driver leaves a pile of boxes about every day.<\/p>\n

Speaking of online ordering\u2026our nearest bigger grocery (think medium sized Kroger or HEB) is a short bus ride away.\u00a0 However, once again, it’s kind of unhandy to carry the bags on the bus.\u00a0 Instead, for less than $10 a month we get unlimited next day delivery of our groceries so long as we buy around $50 of stuff at a time.<\/p>\n

Ordering groceries online is pretty nifty.\u00a0 One challenge for us, though, is that we don’t think in metric very well.\u00a0 When buying a jar of Old El Paso taco sauce (and, yes! They have tons of Old El Paso products in the store) how big is a 226 gram jar?\u00a0 The answer is “not very!”\u00a0 Easy enough when standing in the store, more of a challenge when looking at a picture online.\u00a0 Or, want some black olives?\u00a0 That looks like a nice jar \u2013 450 grams can’t be that much can it?\u00a0 So, we’ve ended up with a small jar of taco sauce, a giant sized jar of black olives, and an abundance of (pureed none the less) canned spinach.\u00a0 Oh well, we’ll put more taco sauce on the next order \u2013 they will bring it right to us.<\/p>\n

Finally, as someone once said, “Britain and America are two nations divided by a common language.”\u00a0 Want to make hamburgers?\u00a0 You want “minced beef” from the store.\u00a0 Jackie wanted to buy some rubbing alcohol for our medicine cabinet.\u00a0 The lady at the pharmacy (or “chemist”) counter looked a bit confused and said they don’t have any alcohol there.\u00a0 After a bit of confusion we discovered that what we wanted was “surgical spirits.”\u00a0 No doubt, this is but the tip of the iceberg.<\/p>\n

Till next time, “cheers!”<\/p>\n
