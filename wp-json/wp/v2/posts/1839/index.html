We have three options for heating our camper:<\/p>\n

    \n
  1. The furnace – which is very inefficient but produces a lot of heat; runs on propane which we always pay for<\/li>\n
  2. The heat pump – part of the air conditioning system but of limited use when the outside air is colder; runs on electricity which we may or may not pay for<\/li>\n
  3. A space heater – amount of heat depends on the unit; and, again, we may or may not pay for the electricity<\/li>\n<\/ol>\n

    The past winters we have used a combination of the three. \u00a0However, in our case we generally spend the winter months in places where the electric service is part of the campsite, so using electric heat makes a lot of sense for us. \u00a0We’ve had \u00a0“cube” heaters\u00a0that help a lot but still leave the 5th wheel feeling a bit drafty and with uneven heat.<\/p>\n

    On the RV forums I kept reading\u00a0about the\u00a0Lasko 755320 Ceramic Tower<\/strong> and how happy people are with it so we decided to give one a try. \u00a0You can find them at several retailers or online. \u00a0The tower has a digital readout, can be programmed, has a timer, and rotates. \u00a0It also comes with a remote control. \u00a0When we got ours it was 68 degrees in the camper – that running our little space heater and with the heat pump set on 72 and cycling on and off. \u00a0Within a half hour the Lasko had raised the temperature to 75.<\/p>\n

    I’m thinking we’ll remove the space heaters from service all together; although we may keep one as a backup and to supplement the heat on especially cold mornings. \u00a0At this point, I recommend this unit to RVers who are looking for alternative heating solutions.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
