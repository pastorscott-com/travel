Bastrop is a few miles southeast of Austin, Texas and it boasts one of the more popular state parks.\u00a0 It has some big features: a large pool and a golf course for starters.\u00a0 It is also in what is called the Lost Pines forest of Texas.\u00a0 East Texas has mile after mile of natural pine tree forests but the Lost Pines are separated by around 100 miles from all of that, making it the most westerly stand of this particular pine tree.\u00a0 This is, as you can guess, a shady park!<\/p>\n

There are many campsites ranging from walk in camp sites to full hook up RV sites to cabins.\u00a0 Our visit is on Memorial Day weekend and the park is at full occupancy.\u00a0 We\u2019re in the full hookup campground which boasts not only full hookups, but all pull through sites to boot.\u00a0 The negative is that many of the sites aren\u2019t as level as most RV\u2019ers want.\u00a0 Those with big rigs may want to do a drive through before settling on a site.\u00a0 The other campgrounds appear to be a bit flatter.\u00a0 All of life is a trade off!<\/p>\n

Our full hookup campground is designed in an interesting way.\u00a0 There\u2019s just one road in and the sites, obviously, are on both sides of the road.\u00a0 The campsites themselves are close together, with your neighbors to front and rear and across the street close by.\u00a0 However, out the front door there\u2019s nothing but forest, giving one a more state park and not commercial park feel.<\/p>\n

We\u2019re camping in central Texas the last of May and it is hot.\u00a0\u00a0 In fact, it was 101 here as I set up.\u00a0 This area can be surprisingly cool or brutally hot \u2013 depending as much on the humidity as on the actual temperature.<\/p>\n

There are some renovations going on here.\u00a0 A new, beautiful playground is about finished and the pool has been redone.\u00a0 Also, each campground is being upgraded to include some wheelchair accessible campsites.\u00a0 There is WIFI but don\u2019t expect to use it from your campsite (at least in this campground).\u00a0 I think it\u2019s at the restrooms which aren\u2019t in the middle of the campground as one might expect but are at the entry, making it a long walk but for all but those in the first campsites.<\/p><\/div>\n
