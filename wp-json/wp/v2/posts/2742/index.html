\u00a0Our 4 1\/2 month stay at San Jacinto Battleground where we have been volunteering on Battleship Texas is drawing to an end.\u00a0 This has been our 4th season here.\u00a0 It is nice feeling we are helping out and the staff always makes us feel appreciated.\u00a0 We also like being close to family and friends during these winter stays.\u00a0 We had more winter this year than we wanted with several cold, icy days.\u00a0 All in all though, we have few complaints; enjoying meeting people and getting to know our fellow camp hosts.<\/p>\n

This year, in addition to our hours volunteering, we have filled in as interim pastor at Baytown Nazarene.\u00a0 The church isn’t far from us and we’ve helped out there the entire time we’ve been at our “winter quarters.”\u00a0 The church family has treated us very well and it has been good getting to know them better.\u00a0 If you add our time filling in at Denison prior to arriving at San Jacinto, I’ve ministered nearly every Sunday over the past 5-6 months.\u00a0 No complaints, but it hasn’t felt very much like retirement to me!\u00a0 Lord willing, I’ll enjoy some down time now that we’re beginning our 2018 Adventure.<\/p>\n

The closer we get to D-Day the more time I spend looking at potential travel routes and campgrounds.\u00a0 Several reservations have already been made; especially at popular campgrounds during the busiest times of the year.\u00a0 \u00a0Our ultimate destination this year is the coast of Maine, but we won’t make it that far till mid- to late summer.\u00a0 As a planner, I enjoy putting the trip together and then refining it. That process will continue all through the Adventure.<\/p>\n

So, it won’t be long before we blast off.\u00a0 We’ll keep posting sightseeing and campground reviews here to the blog.\u00a0 Stay tuned!<\/p>\n
