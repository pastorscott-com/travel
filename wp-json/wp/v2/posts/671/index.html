We’ve all done it.\u00a0 We arrive at the campground ready for a pleasant, enjoyable stay.\u00a0 We find our spot, get settled in and then take a walk.\u00a0 Just across the way, we see it: the best campsite in the entire campground.\u00a0 Almost always it’s occupied but we can’t help but wish it was our camper nestled in that perfect spot with the great view of the lake or the nice big front yard (or maybe just closer to the bathrooms!).<\/p>\n

Before we even know it we’re not as happy with our site as we were just a few minutes ago.\u00a0 We’ve fallen prey to “campsite envy.”<\/p>\n

It’s happened to us often enough that I coined the term.\u00a0 In fact, we developed an unwritten policy: we aren’t going to give in to campsite envy; instead we’re going to appreciate what we have and not let campsite envy diminish our stay.<\/p>\n

Only twice have we pulled up stakes and relocated to a different spot.\u00a0 And, yes, both moves were to considerably superior campsites that just happened to be available.\u00a0 Generally speaking, though, we think it’s better to not get started with the campsite shuffle.\u00a0 For one thing, it’s most often more work than it’s worth.\u00a0 For another, our enjoyment of a campground is seldom centered around our campsite anyway.\u00a0 We enjoy the activities at the campground and sightseeing in the area surrounding the campground.\u00a0 We like meeting the neighbors and sharing a campfire.\u00a0 Who knows?\u00a0 The neighbor you move away from as you give in to campsite envy might have become one of the best friends you’ve ever had!<\/p>\n

So, if it’s early in your stay and the perfect campsite opens up it might make sense to make the move.\u00a0 Otherwise, it’s not that big a deal anyway.\u00a0 Make a note for future reservations and get on with enjoying the campground.\u00a0 Don’t give in to campsite envy!<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
