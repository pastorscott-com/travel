We have enjoyed many stays at Lake Conroe Thousand Trails in Willis, TX.\u00a0 Since I have written several reviews <\/a>of this, our sort of \u201chome\u201d campground, I think I\u2019ll forgo writing yet another.\u00a0 I will, though, mention that this campground continues to receive impressive upgrades.\u00a0 As I understand it, the plan is to make one property in each state into a showplace.\u00a0 Thousand Trails promises that other campgrounds are going to see improvements as time goes by, but for now, the focus is on just a few properties.<\/p>\n

Another project that has been going on during our stay was leveling and resurfacing the pull through sites.\u00a0 Those sites have been the worst in the place for a long time now.\u00a0 The work that is being done will result in considerably better sites.\u00a0 However, they are still back to back parking and, even though they are no longer rutted and rough sites, several are still downhill, either side to side or front to back (or both).\u00a0 I think many people will be disappointed that leveling boards, etc. will still be needed in these pull through sites.<\/p>\n

All in all, though, the improvements to the campground are really nice and will do nothing but make this already popular Thousand Trails even more popular.\u00a0 Since we often begin and end our annual adventures here I\u2019m glad for the work that has been, and continues to be, done.<\/p>\n
