Arrival in Juneau wasn’t till late morning, but overnight we had moved back toward land and by breakfast time there were tree covered mountainous islands on both sides of us.\u00a0 The feeling was something like taking a cruise through the Rocky Mountains!\u00a0 The day was hazy and cloudy but as we got closer to land we could see snow fields and waterfalls.<\/p>\n

We were happy to see that a worship service was scheduled and at 8:00 we made our way to the \u201cShall we Dance\u201d Lounge for church!\u00a0 Our speaker was none other than Joel Olsten of Houston Lakewood Church.\u00a0 However, I must hurry to add that it was on video.\u00a0 Around 30 of us remembered that it was the Lord’s Day and took time for a worship service.<\/p>\n

As we approached land the motion of the sea was replaced once again by the rumble strips we felt in Puget Sound.\u00a0 Also, the land on either side of the ship narrowed the waterway as we continued.\u00a0 It was exciting to near our first port, but the weather was discouraging.\u00a0 The rain and clouds and wind and 57 degrees reminded us that we were, indeed, on the coast of Alaska.<\/p>\n

Two other cruise ships were already in port and the remaining slot looked like it was an afterthought.\u00a0 The ship was brought about broadside into the wind and the captain worked for quite some time to bring the Rhapsody to that dock.\u00a0 However, the wind pushing against our 11+ story vessel won the battle.\u00a0 As we ate an early lunch the ship was backed away and announcements were made that an effort would be made in 45 minutes or so to dock the ship.\u00a0 When the ship’s side thrusters were used at full power the Windjammer vibrated to the point that we wondered if dishes would start breaking.<\/p>\n

An hour or so later we were finally docked.\u00a0 It was announced that those with RCI expeditions were to disembark first, based on their tour times.\u00a0 We \u201cindependent\u201d cruisers could ask for special dispensation to leave the ship, otherwise, the general ship population was to wait.\u00a0 So we waited 45 minutes or so to leave the ship.\u00a0 When our turn came, we paid $3 to be shuttled closer to town where the other ships were docked.
