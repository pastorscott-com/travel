There are some never ending debates in the RV world.\u00a0 For instance, among avid RVers toss out the question, “Fifth wheel or motorhome?” and you’ll find yourself in an opinionated discussion.\u00a0 Ask the fifth wheel crowd, “How big of a truck do I need?” and the rest of the evening will be filled with discussion.<\/p>\n

And, if you want to have some fun, ask “What is a fulltime RVer?” and you are in for an entertaining conversation.\u00a0 The crux of the matter is whether just living in a RV makes one a fulltimer.\u00a0 Obviously, there’s a difference between living behind your grandmother’s house in their 1947 Airstream which is sitting up on concrete blocks and traveling around the country in a high dollar RV.\u00a0 The question is, does the first person get to wear the “fulltimer” label?<\/p>\n

I think the real problem is that the term “fulltimer” needs a qualifying adjective.<\/p>\n

Think of the different kinds of fulltimers there are.\u00a0 At one extreme there’s that person living in the 50 year old camper on the concrete blocks.\u00a0 Then we’ll find people in more current rigs spending their winters in the sunny south and their summers in the moderate north, moving back and forth between campsites they may even own.\u00a0 There are people who follow their jobs, moving when the work moves (and people who are workcamping from place to place).\u00a0 At the other extreme are those who are touring the country.\u00a0 Beyond all that, somewhere in the mix are people who are dreaming of becoming fulltimers and those who have had lots of fulltiming adventures but have now hung up the keys.<\/p>\n

I propose that we just invite all of them into the fulltimers’ tent (not to offend you tenters who are reading this).\u00a0 Then, when we want to be more specific, we add an adjective that clarifies just what sort of fulltimers we are at the present time.\u00a0 In my examples above I’ve mentioned several kinds of fulltimers:<\/p>\n

    \n
  • Stationary<\/li>\n
  • Seasonal<\/li>\n
  • Working<\/li>\n
  • Touring<\/li>\n
  • Wannabe<\/li>\n
  • Usedtobe<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    I’m not trying to do a complete list here – rather, to move the discussion in a positive direction.\u00a0 If you are a fulltimer, what adjective would you use to describe your fulltiming approach?<\/p>\n
