<\/a>Sam Houston Jones State Park is located about 15 minutes north of I10 near Lake Charles, LA.\u00a0 We arrived on a hot July afternoon after driving 400 miles from Pensacola, FL.\u00a0 The campground is filled with big trees which provide abundant shade.\u00a0 It’s a study of contrasts.\u00a0 On the positive side: full hookups, free wifi, and paved camp sites.\u00a0 On the negative side: whoever planned this campground was likely never in a camper.\u00a0 A person might get lucky and land in a site with all the hookups conveniently close at hand.\u00a0 However, that is unlikely.\u00a0 The sewer, water, and electric hookups are most often 20 feet or more from the site.\u00a0 I told the young lady at the office that I couldn’t reach the electric outlet and she produced a 30 foot extension I could borrow.\u00a0 Actually, she had two left.\u00a0 The first was shorted out and the second worked fine.\u00a0 If anyone came in after me, they were out of luck.\u00a0 I was able to reach the sewer by hooking together the two hoses I carry.\u00a0 Then, I couldn’t reach the water.\u00a0 The office carried no hoses, so I walked up to a campsite in which several were sitting in a circle and asked if I could borrow a water hose for the night.\u00a0 Typical of campers, three people offered me, a stranger to them, a hose.\u00a0 With that I was in business.\u00a0 Aside from these things, and the sites being quite close together for a state park this is a nice place to stay.\u00a0 Oh yes, one more thing, we are parked right next to restroom facilities, but the building is closed.\u00a0 As I said, this place is a study of contrasts.\u00a0 The other restroom is not only open, but it’s the only air conditioned restroom I’ve ever seen in a state park!<\/p>\n
