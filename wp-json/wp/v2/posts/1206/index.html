Our main destination today was the Chataue de Versailles. \u00a0We took the Metro to the RER (the light rail of Paris) and then 30 or 40 minutes out to Versailles. \u00a0It was nearly lunch time so we grabbed a light sandwich and then walked the few blocks to the palace. \u00a0Our Paris Museum pass let us go through the short (non existent) line right into the palace grounds. \u00a0In a few minutes we, and many thousand of our friends, were touring the King Louis XIV’s house. \u00a0Photography is allowed, but no flash pictures. \u00a0Since many of the rooms are rather dark, and since there are so many people there, it’s hard to get good interior photos.<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/div>\n

I concentrated on the ceilings for photos. \u00a0They are each a masterpiece \u00a0of their own, each telling a story and making a statement (most generally about how great a person Louis XIV was). \u00a0He believed it was his right and duty as King to show the rest of the world just how good life could be and, because he was quite a party animal, the palace was quite an active place. \u00a0A couple of generations later the French people would vote against this philosophy right in this same place as they stormed the gates, grabbing some of the nice things their\u00a0royalty\u00a0had accumulated and bidding their\u00a0royalty\u00a0“adieu.”<\/p>\n

<\/div>\n

We got audio guides, included in the price. \u00a0I decided I’d try English – and it turned out to be a good choice since I wouldn’t have understood any of the other 7 or 8 languages they offered. \u00a0Seriously, we really liked using the guides, they explain each room in 3 or 4 minutes. \u00a0That makes the experience more interesting.<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/div>\n

Having seen the inside we opted to not pay for a shuttle ride to some of the other buildings, but instead, to look over the gardens around the palace. \u00a0Really, a person could just spend their time at Versailles doing that. \u00a0We didn’t see the fountain run, but it was interesting to remember that during Louis XIV’s day he would send out servants to hand pump the fountains so his guests could look out the windows of the Hall of Mirrors and be impressed by the view. \u00a0These days, the fountains run at specified times, maybe the servants unionized.<\/p>\n

<\/div>\n
<\/a><\/div>\n

Leaving the palace, we walked back into town. \u00a0I enjoyed snapping some shots of Paris as we rode our double decker commuter train back. \u00a0We’re a bit leg weary, so we’re taking a few hours of downtime before venturing out to find some supper later on.<\/p>\n
