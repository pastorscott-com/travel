A lot of people ask for advice on downsizing in preparation for fulltiming.\u00a0 Given that everyone’s specific circumstances are unique, there’s no one size fits all approach to this but I can tell you what we did.<\/p>\n

Early on in the process we just did a more thorough than usual spring cleaning.\u00a0 It’s amazing how much stuff we accumulate through the years that just needs to be put on the curb.\u00a0 For us the focus was on the shed and the garage.<\/p>\n

As we moved forward, we picked a little used room in the house and emptied it out.\u00a0 It became our “sorting room” where we began to put things we knew we didn’t intend on keeping.\u00a0 We also cleared a wall in the garage for stuff we didn’t want in the house, but intended to get rid of.\u00a0 We worked through each room of the house, moving items into the sorting room, more or less putting them into boxes with similar things.\u00a0 That room got surprisingly full.\u00a0 One key to this process is, I think, at first, if you don’t know what to do with an item, just leave it and move to something you do know you don’t want.\u00a0 That stops you from getting constantly sidetracked.\u00a0 It’s kind of interesting, but as the house began to empty, some things that froze us in our tracks were much easier to deal with when they were all that was left in a closet or room.<\/p>\n

We also invited family to put their claim on items they wanted.\u00a0 Those items stayed in place, but we knew they were spoken for.<\/p>\n

As we got to a more serious level, we began to put larger items on Craigslist.\u00a0 I also created a custom group of local friends on Facebook and posted those items there.\u00a0 Being in a metro area probably helped, but a lot of stuff went out the door.\u00a0 Usually, items sold for about half what they would have cost new.\u00a0 We were much more interested in downsizing than we were in making money.\u00a0 Our bicycles, couches, and dinette made some people quite happy.\u00a0 Selling them made us happy too.<\/p>\n

At that point we took another room, now empty, and made it our “holding room.”\u00a0 Items we knew we were going to keep (plus those promised to family) were moved into that room.\u00a0 The house was starting to feel pretty empty.<\/p>\n

It was now time for the big garage sale.\u00a0 All the items in the “sorting room” were priced at yard sale prices and we staged everything for the sale.\u00a0 A LOT of stuff walked out the door over those two days.\u00a0 We concluded the sale by loading all that was left and heading for Goodwill.<\/p>\n

Family was given a deadline for getting their stuff, a few items (winter clothes, photo albums, and the like) went to a family member’s attic. We moved into the 5th wheel and our new, less-cluttered-with-belongings life began.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
