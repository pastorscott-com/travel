One of the most helpful things I did was create a Google Map of our trip. \u00a0I found all the major places we wanted to visit and added place markers for them. \u00a0Under “traffic” I clicked on “transit” and it showed me all the tube lines and stops. \u00a0That helped me visualize where one place was in relation to another and also where the tube stops were.<\/p>\n

The neatest thing, though, was that I could zoom into street view and “walk” down the streets I was going to visit. \u00a0More than once, as we literally walked along a street I had a sense of D\u00e9j\u00e0 vu because I had taken virtual walks via my Google Map.<\/p>\n

There was also a practical side to this. \u00a0When we were debating doing the London Walks Cotswolds tour I got on Trip Advisor and found out what towns were would likely visit. \u00a0I was able to virtually walk around the towns to see if the tour was our cup of tea (it was, very much so).<\/p>\n
