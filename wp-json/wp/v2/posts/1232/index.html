Our Wednesday was spent at sea and the rocking and rolling continued.\u00a0 However it was only mildly objectionable.\u00a0 Again, my objection was that the itinerary showed us being in the inside passage for this part of the cruise.\u00a0 When I booked the cruise one of the negatives for this particular cruise was that it went out to sea as it traveled north in the first days of the trip.\u00a0 I decided I’d risk the possible sea sickness to get north as quickly as possible and then enjoy the inside passage the rest of the trip.\u00a0 Instead, in addition to the journey north, it seemed we were out of sight of land at every opportunity.\u00a0 Thankfully, Jackie and I weren’t suffering from sea sickness.\u00a0 Still, though, the constant motion was somewhat disorienting and, instead of watching the beautiful green mountains and occasional wildlife, we had the rolling sea with whitecaps in all directions.<\/p>\n

This was an interesting stage of the cruise, as people had pretty much figured out what is what on the ship.\u00a0 Rather than exploring the ship, one could pick an area and go straight to it.\u00a0\u00a0 For instance, I settled on the 11th deck with its large, ceiling to floor slanted windows as my favorite hangout.\u00a0 Sitting here I could see a great distance.\u00a0\u00a0 The ocean view highlight of the day for me was seeing two dolphin crest in perfect unity right next to the ship.
