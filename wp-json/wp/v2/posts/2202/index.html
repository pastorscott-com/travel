We’re just concluding our third winter of volunteering at San Jacinto Battlefield and Battleship Texas<\/a>. \u00a0Honestly, my previous reviews pretty much say it all. \u00a0Once again we’ve enjoyed spending most of our volunteer time talking to people about the Texas – the only remaining battleship that served in both WWI and WWII. \u00a0We’ve also enjoyed working with other volunteers who, like us, fulltime in RVs. \u00a0Some are have come here year after year, so when we return it is a bit of a homecoming. \u00a0Others are new friends and it has been fun getting to know them. \u00a0The park staff are a pleasure to work with too. \u00a0If you haven’t gotten the message: we like doing this and plan on returning next fall for another winter of volunteering.<\/p>\n

As has already been described<\/a>, 2016 was a non-traveling year for us. \u00a0Because of that, we’re looking forward to a return to our touring fulltime RV life. \u00a0Our stationary circumstances left me with even more time to plan our 2017 Adventure than I would have had otherwise. \u00a0We have a potential route planned and several reservations have already been made. \u00a0No doubt, we will end up making adjustments, but if you don’t have a plan<\/a> it’s impossible to change your plans! \u00a0Also, we’re headed for some popular areas in the coming months and without reservations we’d end up struggling to find a suitable spot<\/a> or end up paying premium prices somewhere.<\/p>\n

One change we’re making this year is adding a couple of extended, one month stays. \u00a0The idea is to spend extra time in interesting areas, save a bit of money by paying monthly rather than weekly or daily rates, and stay in southern states till warmer temperatures arrive in northern states later in the spring. \u00a0Time will tell if we like this strategy and will include it in future planning.<\/p>\n

We’ve had a good stay at our winter quarters of San Jacinto Battleground and Battleship Texas and we’re looking forward to great days on our 2017 Adventure.<\/p>\n
