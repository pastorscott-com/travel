For Sunday our intention was to take a breather from sightseeing and make it a day of worship and rest.\u00a0 We started our day at Westminster Abbey at their 10:00 worship service.\u00a0 We got there just after they closed entry to the part of the church where the main service was taking place and ended up sitting in the \u201cNave\u201d with 20 or so other people.\u00a0 We could see through into the service well enough to see the men and boy\u2019s choir as well as see the clergyman as he delivered a Sunday sermon. \u00a0Also, the organ was beautiful. \u00a0Every inch of the church has statues, etc. but we were able to focus on worship.\u00a0 There\u2019s something powerful about stating the Apostle\u2019s Creed in the place where it has been said for over 1000 years.\u00a0 Also, the singing of the Lord\u2019s Prayer was deeply moving.\u00a0 The sermon was quite good and we came away feeling we had genuinely worshiped.\u00a0 To keep tourists from coming to church just to keep from paying a tour fee they have a strict \u201cno pictures\u201d policy on Sundays.\u00a0 It\u2019s quite understandable but we wish we had been able to take at least a couple of photos to help us remember the meaningful worship service.<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/div>\n

For lunch we headed over to Greenwich market.\u00a0 However, the crowds were so large that we walked though just to see what the food and other venders had and then, out on the street we spotted a real English Fish and Chips restaurant.\u00a0 We split a meal and had food left over!\u00a0 We walked over the the Maritime Museum that we visited the day we arrived.\u00a0 At that time we were sleep deprived that we only saw a small part of it.\u00a0 Today we saw a bit more before making the short jump back to Canary Wharf.<\/p>\n

This area of London is considered to be the \u201cWall Street\u201d of London with skyscrapers and lots of people in suits hurrying around.\u00a0 The tube and the DLR or \u201clight rail\u201d intersect here as well as public transport via the river Thames.\u00a0 Our 7 or 8 minute walk to public transportation takes us across a nifty bridge and into a large shopping mall surrounded by office buildings.\u00a0 We\u2019re taking it easy through the afternoon here at the half way mark of our trip.<\/p>\n
