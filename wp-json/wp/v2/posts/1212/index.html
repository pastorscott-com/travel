We headed out for another day of sightseeing in London.\u00a0 Let me mention that the weather has been unseasonably warm here\u2026highs in the upper 70\u2019s are summer like for this area.\u00a0 It\u2019s delightful to be outdoors, not so much on the tube or on the bus.<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/div>\n

We started our day by heading for Buckingham Palace and the Changing of the Guard.\u00a0 The pageantry is quite impressive, taking about an hour.\u00a0 We found a spot on the rail around the monument, facing the palace.\u00a0 It was a real good spot to see it all.\u00a0 The only better place would have been along the tall iron fence looking right at the courtyard.\u00a0 There, you would miss some of the parade, but you\u2019d see and the saluting and such as well as be able to better hear the music from the marching band.\u00a0 All in all, though, ours was a satisfying experience.<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/div>\n

From Buckingham we wanted to travel a good distance to the British Museum.\u00a0 We opted to ride a double decker bus all the way from Victoria Station to the museum.\u00a0 It was quite a ride.\u00a0 Listen to me very carefully here: you don\u2019t want to drive in London.\u00a0 It\u2019s beyond impossible!\u00a0 Still, sitting in the upstairs front seat is worth the ride and I highly recommend it as a way to see the city from an entirely different perspective.<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/div>\n

Our afternoon was spent at the British Museum.\u00a0 It\u2019s an amazing place.\u00a0 We spent a great deal of time in the Egyptology area, checking out everything from the Rosetta Stone to seeing several mummies.\u00a0 The Greek and Roman exhibitions are just as impressive.\u00a0 We walked and looked and sat and rested and walked and looked some more.<\/p>\n

Since we enjoyed our bus ride to the museum, and since we knew the tube would be packed and hot, we opted to ride the bus out of the downtown area and at least part of the way back out to Canary Warf.\u00a0 It started out as a stroke of genius.\u00a0 We got a front seat and watched the city go by.\u00a0 Things went well till we were about 20 minutes from where we needed to switch to light rail.\u00a0 Then, everything ground to a halt.\u00a0 We never did see what happened, but we sat still watching motorcyclists ride between cars and buses, having to weave back and forth to keep from hitting mirrors and bicyclists who must have a death wish riding right with them \u2013 and nothing else moving.\u00a0 Finally, people started getting off the bus and we followed suit.\u00a0 It was only then that we realized that the tube we ride nearly everyday had a stop about 3 blocks from where we had sat for so long.\u00a0 We crowded in and were back at our stop in about 10 minutes.<\/p>\n
