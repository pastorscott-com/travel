Our destination today was Windsor Castle.\u00a0 It takes another train ride of about an hour to get there.\u00a0 Let me say that the British trains are very good.\u00a0 They are smooth and quiet.\u00a0 The seats are a bit narrow, but aside from that, they are a great way to travel.\u00a0 The trains are so quiet that people talk to one another (of course, no eye contact or conversations with strangers!) in subdued tones.\u00a0 As we did on our way to the Cotswolds we rode in a \u201cquiet car\u201d \u2013 no cell phones or music.\u00a0 It was a restful, interesting ride out to and back from Windsor.<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/div>\n

From either of the Windsor train stations it\u2019s a short walk up to the castle.\u00a0 It\u2019s an amazing place to visit.\u00a0 The castle has been there for centuries, but the walls are like new.\u00a0 The Queen doesn\u2019t allow pictures to be taken inside of any building, so I snapped away while outside, but put the camera away as we viewed the Queen\u2019s dollhouse (filled with miniatures \u2013 it even has a working plumbing system).\u00a0 If you\u2019ve never heard of it, google it \u2013 it\u2019s really neat.\u00a0 Then we took a long and interesting walk through the royal apartments.\u00a0 Every room has portraits of famous members of the royal families painted by famous artists.\u00a0 The ceilings are works of art, the furniture pieces are all works of art; there\u2019s gold and silver, weapon displays, banners, and lots of other impressive stuff.\u00a0 Some of the rooms are basically part of a museum (\u201cNapoleon slept here\u201d), while others are actually used for state activities.\u00a0 Right now the Queen isn\u2019t in residence there, but will be shortly.\u00a0 That\u2019s actually a good thing because we were able to view some rooms that won\u2019t be available to the public once she arrives.<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/div>\n

On the property is another 1000 year old church, St. George\u2019s Chapel.\u00a0 Again, no photos, but it\u2019s quite a place to visit.\u00a0 Lots of kings and queens and other royals are buried in the church, and the Quire (I\u2019d call it the \u201csanctuary\u201d) has seats for the \u201cKnights of Windsor.\u201d\u00a0 There are shields for all the knights over their seats, and then, there\u2019s an impressive sword and shield for the current knight who claims his place at the church.<\/p>\n

We stayed for the Evensong service and just happened to be there for a special memorial service for one of the Windsor Knights who recently passed away.\u00a0 We heard another all male choir (the boy sopranos in particular were impressive).\u00a0 However, the most interesting thing was the processional of around twelve Knights of Windsor.\u00a0 These are all older men, mostly heroes from WWII.\u00a0 They were dressed in what reminded me of Admiral outfits: red coats with tails, feathered hats, etc.\u00a0 These men have all had distinguished military careers now and serve St. Georges.\u00a0 There was lots of scripture and singing (obviously, quite \u201chigh church\u201d) and a sort of memorial sermon by the man\u2019s son.\u00a0 We would have felt like intruders except for the fact that we, once again, found ourselves sitting in the Nave rather than in the Quire.\u00a0 We could see through the opening into the service and hear everything quite well, but were actually sitting outside the service.\u00a0 As I understand it, normally visitors are invited into the main sanctuary for the daily Evensong service but since there were many dignitaries there those seats were reserved for them.\u00a0 To tell you the truth, I don\u2019t think Jackie or I would have wanted to be inside anyway, we were way underdressed for the occasion as I left my Admiral suit home on this trip.<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/div>\n

And yes \u2013 we got the obligatory photo with the guard.\u00a0 He\u00a0wasn’t\u00a0very polite.\u00a0 I said \u201cthanks\u201d and he didn\u2019t even blink an eye!\u00a0 If you ever come to London \u2013 don\u2019t miss Windsor \u2013 it\u2019s a \u201cmust see.\u201d<\/p>\n
