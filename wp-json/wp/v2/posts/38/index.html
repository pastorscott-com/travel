Location: Clear Lake, Texas<\/p>\n

Freeman is part of the\u00a0Harris County Public Library\u00a0<\/a>System located \u00a0in Clear Lake, TX. \u00a0This is the library I retired from so I am some what prejudiced toward it.\u00a0Freeman is one of the larger branches and is able to offer programs and classes not available in some of the smaller branches. \u00a0They have free computer classes, regular teen programs, programs for children of all ages as well as\u00a0programs geared for adults on a weekly basis. \u00a0There are two floors with the children’s department and circulation desk on the first floor. Also on the first floor are several internet computers as well as a large screen TV with seating nearby. The second floor has about 30 public\u00a0access computers,\u00a0the adult area of fiction, non-fiction and reference materials. There is also a large supervised teen area set aside for young people to study and go online and a computer lab that is open to the public when there are no computer classes in session.<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/p>\n

When entering the library one of the first things that greets you is a wall sized mural
\n<\/a>showing the history of the Clear Lake area from pirates to aerospace. If \u00a0you look closely at the mural you will see \u00a0that the artist included staff members and County representatives in the art work. This beautiful work was done by\u00a0Pat Rawlings<\/a>\u00a0who has many more\u00a0interesting\u00a0works of art \u00a0on his website that is linked above.<\/p>\n
