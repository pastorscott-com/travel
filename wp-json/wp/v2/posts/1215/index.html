Monday night came early and we were glad for it.\u00a0 We went to bed about 8:00 local and 2:00 \u201chome time.\u201d\u00a0 After going pretty much without sleep overnight Sunday and then hitting the ground running for a day of sightseeing, we struggled to stay awake that late.\u00a0 Jackie pretty much slept all night.\u00a0 I conked out for about 4 hours and then woke up.\u00a0 I\u2019m no fan of sleeping pills, but I took one and then slept the rest of the night.\u00a0 We both woke around 8:00am feeling refreshed and ready for the day.<\/p>\n

We walked a few blocks to the Canary Wharf pier and to the Thames Clipper into the heart of London, passing under Tower Bridge and seeing many of the famous sights of London from the water.<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/div>\n

After taking some photos of Big Ben and Parliament we walked over to the Churchill War Rooms and Museum.\u00a0 It was here that Winston Churchill lead England through WWII.\u00a0 It\u2019s quite an interesting place and we surprised ourselves by staying about 2 hours.<\/p>\n

We grabbed some sandwiches from a shop that is part of a chain, Tesco Express.\u00a0 Prepackaged sandwiches, chips and drinks.\u00a0 We ate, sitting on a wall with many other locals and tourists, right across the street from Big Ben.<\/p>\n

<\/div>\n

From there we hopped on the tube headed for our next destination.\u00a0 It\u2019s easy to see why Londoners have a love\/hate relationship with the tube.\u00a0 You can go about anywhere on the tube and trains are quite frequent, generally less than 5 minutes apart.\u00a0 However, the trains are very crowded and not air conditioned.\u00a0 On one journey we made people were packed in, standing room only.\u00a0 Hardly anyone speaks and everyone reads the paper or a book \u2013 I guess it\u2019s a way to isolate oneself in such an uninsolated situation.<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/div>\n

Our other big stop of the day was the British Library.\u00a0 One room, in particular is worth visiting.\u00a0 It is full of rare books, etc.\u00a0 including 400 year old Bibles, original Shakespearen manuscripts, and one of the earliest handwritten copies of Handel\u2019s Messiah.<\/p>\n

We found our way back to Canary Wharf, the Wall Street of London.\u00a0 It also has a huge shopping mall.\u00a0 At one end we found a big grocery store and bought some supper to take back to the flat.<\/p>\n
