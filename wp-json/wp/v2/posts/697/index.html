The sure-fired, guaranteed, spirited discussion topic among fulltiming RVers is, well you know what it is: which is best for fulltiming, motorhome or 5th<\/sup> wheel.\u00a0 The obvious thing is that both are quite suitable.\u00a0 After all, there are thousands upon thousands of people happily and enthusiastically traveling and living in both.<\/p>\n

Note: after 6 years in the 5th wheel we did make the move to the motorhome. Here’s my article comparing the two.<\/a><\/p>\n

Still, there are advantages and disadvantages to both.\u00a0 Just for fun, here are my comparisons (note: I’ll update this list when I come across more “pros” either way):<\/p>\n

Motorhome<\/b><\/p>\n

    \n
  1. Easier to park and set up \u2013 important for people who move often or are challenged by such things. One thing that helps is that the driver is sitting right up front over the wheels – and the steering on a mothorhome lets the driver turn the rig very sharply<\/li>\n
  2. Lends itself to the famous \u201cin motion passenger pit stop\u201d and it\u2019s less famous cousin, the \u201choney grab me a soda from the fridge\u201d in motion request<\/li>\n
  3. Better for \u201cbugging out\u201d from a rest area or Walmart parking lot if things sound a bit strange outside<\/li>\n
  4. If you tow a car you have a small, economical daily driver \u2013 also nice for scouting out the campground for sites upon arrival<\/li>\n
  5. Most comfortable travel vehicle<\/li>\n
  6. It provides the famous, \u201cwe don\u2019t have to even go outside if it\u2019s raining when we arrive\u201d advantage<\/li>\n
  7. While there are more steps to get inside, once inside it is all one level<\/li>\n<\/ol>\n

    5th<\/sup> Wheel<\/b><\/p>\n

      \n
    1. More room inside \u2013 important for people who stay in one place longer<\/li>\n
    2. The \u201chouse\u201d and the vehicle are separate \u2013 really important when the vehicle has to go in the shop \u2013 you don\u2019t have to get a motel room for, say, a transmission rebuild<\/li>\n
    3. Cost of maintenance on tow vehicle is generally less \u2013 doesn\u2019t require \u201cbig rig\u201d service oil changes, and camper tires are less expensive<\/li>\n
    4. Depending on your taste, startup costs are generally less<\/li>\n
    5. Greater variety of floor plans<\/li>\n
    6. More stable when driving in windy conditions (assuming a well matched tow vehicle and 5th wheel)<\/li>\n
    7. Propane tanks can be removed and taken to a refill station<\/li>\n
    8. When you want to upgrade you can upgrade the tow vehicle and 5th wheel separetly rather than having to greater expense of trading motorhomes<\/li>\n
    9. A 5th wheel is easier to level on sites that are unlevel front to back – you don’t have to worry about putting the front tires up on blocks or having them dangling in the air if using an automatic leveling system<\/li>\n
    10. Here’s a real biggie: a husband towing a\u00a05th wheel\u00a0is somewhat less likely to drive off without the wife aboard. \ud83d\ude42<\/li>\n<\/ol>\n

      Here\u2019s our story: when we began our search we set a budget for our fulltiming rig.\u00a0 We started off looking mostly at diesel motorhomes but soon became discouraged that the nice ones were over our budget.\u00a0 We began pricing bigger pickups and 5th<\/sup> wheels.\u00a0 It became apparent to us that we had a better chance of staying under our budget while getting something we liked if we went the 5th<\/sup> wheel route \u2013 and we did.<\/p>\n

      A big influence on us was the living space available in the 5th<\/sup> wheels as well as the better floor plans.\u00a0 We wanted a normal living room set up, including a more natural location of the television.\u00a0 Most motorhomes we looked at didn\u2019t offer such a floor plan.\u00a0 A lot of 5th<\/sup> wheels didn\u2019t either, but some did.<\/p>\n

      Now that we’re fulltiming, I admit that there have been times when I wished for a motorhome\/towed vehicle.\u00a0 The truck uses a lot of fuel, towing or not.\u00a0 When we arrive at a campground, I really dislike driving around with the 5th<\/sup> wheel in tow while I cruise up and down narrow roads trying to pick out a campsite.<\/p>\n

      Most of the time, though, I\u2019m convinced we made a good choice.\u00a0 We like the living space the 5th<\/sup> wheel provides.\u00a0 We think some of the advantages of the motorhome are minor at best.\u00a0 After all, we would have to stop motorhome or 5th<\/sup> wheel for the driver to make a pit stop – not to mention that walking around in a motorhome in motion is somewhat dangerous and likely illegal.\u00a0 We haven\u2019t had to set up in any downpours, and if we did, we\u2019d just get a little wet moving from the pickup to the camper where we\u2019d wait it out.\u00a0 Also, we don\u2019t do camp Walmart or stay in rest areas so the possibility of needing to make a run for it from some undesirable situation really isn\u2019t that great.<\/p>\n

      Then, we\u2019ve had the pickup in the shop three times now, once overnight.\u00a0 It was nice not having to take our whole house in to the shop and vacate it just for new radiator hoses!<\/p>\n

      Maybe, someday, we\u2019ll join the motorhome crowd.\u00a0 When we do, we\u2019ll enjoy some of the advantages of living and traveling in a motorhome.\u00a0 At the same time, though, I think we\u2019ll miss some of the pluses of our 5th<\/sup> wheel.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
