Luggage and cruise documents in hand we arrived at Seattle’s Pier 91.\u00a0 Friends from the Seattle area hosted us for a few days and then were kind enough to drop us off at the pier.\u00a0 As soon as the vehicle stopped a porter asked us if we were on Rhapsody of the Seas.\u00a0 When we said we were he said he’d be glad to take care of our baggage for us.\u00a0 Since our booking was all electronic, we didn’t have official luggage tags that some people have.\u00a0 The porter led us to a counter where a man asked our names and then handed our porter tags.\u00a0 We stood there while he put them on and then he told us that was it.\u00a0 The bags would be delivered to our room.\u00a0 I handed him a tip and we went inside where a smiling lady looked at our documentation and pointed us to security.<\/p>\n

Passing through security was a lot like going through security at an airport.\u00a0 However, unlike it is at the airport, the people handing security were smiling and welcoming us.\u00a0 From there we stood in a long, but fast moving line for cruise check in.\u00a0 Within 10 minutes we were at a window where we showed our passports, cruise documents, and the credit card which was linked to our sail account.\u00a0 Then, with our sail passes in hand (these serve as our identification, room key, and on board credit card) we walked up several flights to board the ship on level five.\u00a0 Around 20 minutes from arriving at the pier we walked onto the ship.<\/p>\n

The rooms were already open so we found our way to our tiny room.\u00a0 Since we’d asked for a \u201cqueen size\u201d bed the room had been set up that way.\u00a0 The room was so small that you couldn’t walk around the bed, but had to climb on and off the end.\u00a0 Closet space was adequate in spite of the fact that our two big suitcases took up most of one side.\u00a0 The bathroom was tiny and the shower had only a curtain rather than a door.\u00a0 It is likely that the biggest advantage to having a higher class cabin isn’t the outside view so much as it is the amount of space.\u00a0 Still, there was plenty of drawer space.\u00a0 We unloaded our luggage and found spots for everything.<\/p>\n

We had eaten while waiting for the luggage to be delivered.\u00a0 The buffet restaurant is called the Windjammer and is located in the front of the ship on deck nine.\u00a0 Our first meal there wasn’t especially impressive; however, subsequent meals were just fine.
