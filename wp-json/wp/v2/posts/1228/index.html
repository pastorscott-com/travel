\n Cruising to Alaska on Rhapsody of the Seas – Pros<\/b>
\nScenery<\/b> – awesome
\nWildlife<\/b> – good, but we didn’t see enough, probably should have sprung for a tour
\nMeals onboard<\/b> – almost always good, sometimes excellent
\nService onboard <\/b>– friendly and capable
\nMusic onboard<\/b> – good and sometimes excellent
\nPorts<\/b> – interesting and sometimes unforgettable
\nChange of itinerary<\/b> – trading wind and rain in Skagway for beautiful Endicott Arm, worth it<\/p>\n

Cruising to Alaska on Rhapsody of the Seas – Cons<\/b>
\nChange of itinerary<\/b> – it felt like we traded cruising the Inside Passage to being out of sight of land for gambling, yuck!
\nCoffee<\/b> – made double strong all the time, almost made me a tea drinker
\nCabin<\/b> – I knew I was getting an inside cabin, but never dreamed I would have to crawl off and on the end of the bed
\nSneaky fees<\/b> – park the ship at the most distant pier and then wait in line and pay an extra fee for a shuttle to where we were supposed to be in the first place<\/p>\n
