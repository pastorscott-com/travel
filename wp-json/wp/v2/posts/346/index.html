\u00a0The other highlight of our time here was a visit to Yorktown.\u00a0 We began our visit at the Yorktown National Park Visitor center where we watched and introductory film that helped us understand the driving tour. \u00a0We were surprised to see how close the two armies actually were as they prepared for to do battle. \u00a0The fact that Washington was able to march his forces, build the redoubts and do battle show determination and stamina on the part of the Colonials. \u00a0Of course, having the French fleet hold off the British Navy was a turning point, contributing to Cornwallis\u2019 inability to retreat across the York River to Gloucester.<\/p>\n

During the driving tour we saw the restored Moore House where the terms for the surrender of Yorktown were worked out. \u00a0It’s a lovely home originally on 500 acres with a view of the York River.<\/p>\n

Finishing our trip we rode the free trolley from the NPS visitors center to Yorktown and walked along Water Street by the River then took the trolley back.<\/p>\n
