<\/a>We\u2019ve been out in the camper for a week now, three moves, and we\u2019ve enjoyed having satellite TV every day.\u00a0 The carryout works pretty good.\u00a0 I think I\u2019ll get better at deploying it as I use it more.\u00a0 Basically, you set it out where you think it will be able to see the southern sky.\u00a0 Hook up the power and the receiver and wait for it to find the satellites.\u00a0 Once they are found picture appears on the TV screen and you start watching TV.<\/p>\n

When it works like that, well, what more could you ask for?<\/p>\n

Related posts are here.<\/a><\/p>\n

If you put it in the wrong place and it can\u2019t find the satellites \u2013 or if it only finds one and not the other, you wait awhile (and at this point I haven\u2019t figured out how long it\u2019s supposed to be) and then move it to a more likely location, disconnect the power, reconnect the power, and wait again.<\/p>\n

<\/a>Another complication I finally realized is that the coax connector on my new Wineguard is, I think, a bit flaky.\u00a0 With the TV on, receiving the satellite just fine, I can reach around to the back of the receiver and put a bit of pressure on the connector and the receiver will immediately report \u201ccomplete signal loss\u201d \u2013 if I release the pressure the picture is back.\u00a0 I\u2019m not sure, but I think there was at least one time that I kept moving the Carryout, thinking that it wasn\u2019t seeing the satellite when, actually the connection wasn\u2019t quite right.<\/p>\n

Again, as I use the Carryout more I think I\u2019ll have a better idea as to where it\u2019s likely to find the satellites and, once the coax is fixed\/replaced I think I\u2019m going to like it.<\/p>\n
