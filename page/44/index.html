In and Around West Central Indiana

June 15, 2013January 31, 2024 grscott

Click this for full screen photos

While here we in west central Indiana we took a half day trip to see a few of the covered bridges in this area. The ones we saw on Rock Run Creek and Big Raccoon Creek were built in the early 1900‚Ä≤s and are very picturesque. I was more comfortable on other bridges that were restored in a way that didn‚Äôt have that ‚Äúfeature!‚Äù Another difference is that some bridges have large or small window-like openings that look out on the creek below. There is a Victorian living room with a winding stairway and early phonograph player, a room like an old saloon with a wooden Indian, kitchen appliances, a wagon used to deliver goods and communication devices including many early telephones and many signs from the company‚Äôs early days. One display is of particular interest. It is a vault with replicas of gold bars and a desk set up to look like it might have looked in the past with ledgers open for business. There is a coffee shop where Scott bought some Rex brand coffee and I had a cinnamon roll. It tasted wonderful and I would recommend this as a place to visit if you are in the area.
